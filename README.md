# Barbie Character Profiling Analysis 🎀

On this very day, the original **Barbie doll debuted in 1959 at the American International Toy Fair in New York City**. Inspired by this milestone, I decided to study the **character profiling of Barbie dolls that are part of the Barbie Cinematographic Universe**.

The goal of this project is to explore how **Barbie characters evolved over time**, focusing on aspects such as **gender distribution, race/species representation, appearance diversity, and voice actors**.

The dataset used for this project was sourced from Kaggle.

Using **Python-based data analysis and visualization**, several insights were derived from the dataset.

---

# Dataset Overview

The dataset contains information about characters appearing in the **Barbie Cinematographic Universe**, including attributes such as:

- Character name  
- Gender  
- Race / species  
- Skin tone  
- Hair color  
- Voice actors  
- Timeline of character appearances  

This dataset helps analyze how the **Barbie universe expanded across movies and characters**.

---

# Key Findings

## 1. Character Distribution in the Barbie Universe

The analysis shows that the **Barbie character dominates the universe**, appearing approximately **20 times across the dataset**.

Other prominent characters include:

- **Chelsea**
- **Ken**, who appears around **6 times**

This indicates that the Barbie universe primarily revolves around **female characters**.

### Gender Distribution

- **Female characters:** 513  
- **Male characters:** 350  

This confirms that the Barbie universe is **female-dominated**, aligning with the franchise's focus on female storytelling.

### Race / Species Distribution

The dataset also includes different character species.

| Category | Count |
|--------|------|
| Human | 530 |
| Mermaid | 43 |
| Dog | 32 |
| Fairy | 23 |

Overall, **858 characters belong to the Barbie universe**, with the majority being human characters.

---

# 2. Diversity in Character Appearance

The dataset also highlights patterns in **skin tone and hair color representation**.

### Skin Tone Distribution

- **Light / Light Medium:** 410 characters  

This suggests that **visual diversity in skin tones is still somewhat limited** within the Barbie universe.

### Hair Color Distribution

Hair color representation shows the following pattern:

| Hair Color | Count |
|-----------|------|
| Brown | 189 |
| Blonde | 157 |
| Black | 43 |

This indicates that **brown and blonde hair dominate**, while **black hair representation remains comparatively lower**, suggesting limited diversity in character appearance.

---

# 3. Voice Actor Representation

Another interesting dimension analyzed was the **voice actors who portray Barbie characters in films**.

The most frequent voice actors include:

- **Kelly Sheridan**  
- **Kathleen Barr**  
- **Tabitha Germain**  

Among these, **Kelly Sheridan** appears as the primary voice actor for the Barbie character across multiple films.

---

# 4. Timeline of Character Evolution

A timeline analysis was conducted to understand how **character roles evolved across Barbie films**.

The visualization shows several important trends:

### Early Phase
Barbie initially had **centralized roles**, where the storyline mainly revolved around the Barbie character.

### Expansion Phase
Over time, the universe introduced **more supporting characters**, including friends, family members, and companions.

### Diversity Phase
Later films began to **embrace diversity**, introducing characters with different backgrounds and fantasy elements.

### Modern Phase
Recent Barbie films integrate:

- **Multicultural characters**
- **Fantasy elements**
- **More complex supporting roles**

This shows how the Barbie universe evolved from a **single protagonist narrative to a more diverse character ecosystem**.
